<Warning>
  **Roadmap preview.** The `listening` job schema below is accepted and validated
  today — you can author it and it will parse — but Shipfox does **not execute
  listening jobs yet**. A listening job currently acts as a scheduling boundary:
  it doesn't run, and non-listening jobs that depend on it stay blocked. Don't rely
  on this in production until execution ships. This page documents the authored
  shape so you can follow along.
</Warning>

Most jobs run once when a trigger fires. An **event-driven job** instead *listens*:
it reacts to a stream of events over time and stops when a terminating condition is
met. The intent is to model long-lived reactions — draining a queue of events,
watching a resource until it settles — inside a single job.

## The `listening` shape

A job becomes event-driven by adding a `listening` block:

```yaml
jobs:
  watch_deploys:
    listening:
      on:
        - source: deploy_hook
          event: webhook.received
      until:
        - source: deploy_hook
          event: webhook.received
          filter: event.body.status == "stable"
      timeout: "1h"
      max_executions: 20
      batch:
        debounce: "5s"
        max_size: 10
        max_wait: "1m"
      on_resolve: finish
    steps:
      - run: ./reconcile.sh
```

<ParamField path="listening.on" type="trigger[]" required>
  The events the job listens for. Each entry uses the same `{source, event,
  filter?, with?}` shape as a workflow [trigger](/concepts/triggers).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="listening.until" type="trigger[]">
  Events that end the listening job. When one arrives, the job resolves.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="listening.timeout" type="string">
  Maximum wall-clock time to keep listening before the job resolves.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="listening.max_executions" type="number">
  Caps how many times the job reacts before it resolves.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="listening.batch" type="object">
  Aggregates bursts of events: `debounce` (quiet window before reacting),
  `max_size` (events per batch), and `max_wait` (longest a batch waits before
  flushing).
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="listening.on_resolve" type="'finish' | 'cancel'">
  What happens when `until` fires: `finish` completes the job normally (default),
  `cancel` cancels it.
</ParamField>
